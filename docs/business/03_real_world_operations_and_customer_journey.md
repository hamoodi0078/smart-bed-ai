## How the Business Runs in Real Life

This document describes how Smart Bed AI actually operates as a business — not the technology, but the real-world processes, people, and touchpoints that make the product work for customers from day one to year three.

---

## The Full Operational Flow

### Step 1 — Marketing and Awareness
The customer first hears about Smart Bed AI. This happens through:

- **Social media content** (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube Shorts) — video demonstrations of Fajr wake-up, Ramadan mode, the LED sunrise sequence
- **Islamic lifestyle influencers** — creators who talk about sleep, Sunnah routines, and wellness
- **Word of mouth** — a friend or family member who already has the bed
- **Physical showrooms** — furniture stores in Kuwait where the bed is on display
- **Islamic conferences and exhibitions** — Ramadan exhibitions, health fairs, family events in Kuwait and GCC
- **WhatsApp community groups** — a strong channel in the Gulf for product recommendations

The first impression a potential customer gets must immediately answer the question: *"What makes this bed worth paying for?"*

### Step 2 — Consideration and Research
The customer visits the website or product page. They spend time here deciding whether to buy. What they look at:

- Product demo video (the full experience: voice conversation, Fajr wake-up, LED scenes)
- Testimonials from other Gulf users
- Islamic features page — showing exactly how Fajr, Ramadan mode, and Hadith work
- Subscription comparison table (Free vs. Standard vs. Pro)
- FAQ about privacy, installation, and setup difficulty
- WhatsApp chat with a sales agent for personal questions

Trust is the key conversion factor at this stage. The product makes big claims — proof, testimonials, and a clear explanation of how it works are essential.

### Step 3 — Purchase
The customer places an order through the website or directly through a sales agent on WhatsApp.

- **Payment options:** credit/debit card, bank transfer, and buy-now-pay-later (BNPL) through a local Gulf fintech partner
- **Order confirmation:** an automatic email and WhatsApp message is sent immediately
- **Subscription selection:** during checkout, the customer selects their subscription tier (or starts with Free)
- **Lead time:** the customer is informed of the delivery and installation timeline (typically 5–10 business days in Kuwait)

### Step 4 — Manufacturing and Delivery
This is the operational backbone of the hardware side of the business:

- **Assembly:** bed frames are assembled at a local or regional manufacturing partner. Sensor kits and electronics are assembled separately and installed into the frame before shipment.
- **Quality check:** each unit goes through a standard checklist before leaving the facility — sensors tested, LEDs confirmed, voice module active, app connectivity verified
- **Delivery:** a trained delivery team (not a generic courier) delivers the bed to the customer's home
- **In-home delivery:** white-glove service — the team carries the bed to the bedroom, removes packaging, and positions the bed correctly

### Step 5 — Installation
Installation is a key moment. A bad install experience can destroy customer confidence.

The delivery team or a separate installation technician:
1. Assembles the bed frame if it arrives flat-packed
2. Connects the sensor pod, LED strips, and speaker/microphone module
3. Plugs in the power unit and verifies all hardware lights are green
4. Connects the bed to the home's Wi-Fi network using a simple setup process
5. Downloads the Smart Bed AI app on the customer's phone and guides them through account creation
6. Runs a quick hardware test: "Say 'Hey Smart Bed' and ask for today's prayer times"
7. Confirms the subscription tier and activates the account
8. Leaves the customer with a printed quick-start card and QR code linking to support

**Total installation time:** approximately 45–90 minutes depending on room layout and furniture.

### Step 6 — Onboarding
The first 7 days are the most critical for customer retention. If the customer doesn't experience the product's value in the first week, they will churn.

**Day 1 — Welcome and setup:**
- Danah introduces herself and asks the customer's name, prayer preferences, and sleep goals
- The customer is guided through setting their Fajr prayer location and alarm preferences
- First wind-down routine is run that evening

**Day 2–3 — First experience:**
- Customer receives their first morning report: *"Last night you slept for 7 hours and 20 minutes. Your sleep score was 71. Let's improve that together."*
- First daily Hadith notification arrives via WhatsApp
- Danah checks in: *"How did you sleep last night? Was the wake-up gentle enough?"*

**Day 4–7 — Habit formation:**
- Customer configures partner mode (if applicable)
- Customer receives onboarding email series with tips on getting the most from each feature
- A support agent reaches out via WhatsApp to check in and answer questions

**Day 14 — Check-in call (Standard and Pro):**
- A human support agent schedules a 15-minute video or voice call to ensure everything is working
- This is also an opportunity to upsell from Free to Standard or from Standard to Pro

### Step 7 — Daily Use
Once onboarded, the product runs automatically. The customer interacts with it through:
- **Voice** — "Hey Smart Bed, I'm going to sleep now" / "Start my wind-down" / "What's my sleep score?"
- **Mobile app** — checking sleep history, adjusting settings, viewing reports
- **Notifications** — prayer time alerts, sleep reminders, morning health summary, weekly Hadith

The product is designed so that **daily use requires zero effort from the customer.** Danah observes, adapts, and acts. The customer's only job is to sleep.

### Step 8 — Ongoing Support
Problems happen. Hardware can malfunction. Settings can get confusing. Here is how support works:

**Tier 1 — Self-service:**
- In-app FAQ and troubleshooting guide
- Video tutorials covering common setups and questions
- Automated diagnostic: the app can run a self-check and report issues

**Tier 2 — WhatsApp Support:**
- Chat with a support agent (human) during business hours (8 AM – 10 PM Kuwait time)
- Pro subscribers get a priority queue with a target response time of under 30 minutes
- Standard subscribers target under 2 hours

**Tier 3 — Remote Diagnostics:**
- Support team can access a diagnostic read-out from the customer's bed (with consent) to identify sensor or software issues
- Most software problems are resolved remotely — no need for a visit

**Tier 4 — On-Site Visit:**
- For hardware problems that cannot be resolved remotely, a technician visits the home
- Within Kuwait: target response within 48 hours for Pro, 72 hours for Standard
- Hardware replacement is handled under warranty

**Warranty terms (estimated):**
- 1-year hardware warranty included with all purchases
- Extended warranty (2 or 3 years) available as add-on purchase
- Warranty covers manufacturing defects, sensor failures, LED malfunctions
- Does not cover physical damage or water exposure

### Step 9 — Subscription Renewal and Upgrades

**Annual renewal:**
- 30 days before renewal, the customer receives a WhatsApp and email reminder
- Renewal is automatic if the customer enabled auto-renewal at signup
- A retention offer is sent to at-risk customers (those who have not used the product much in recent months): a discount or a free month extension

**Upgrade flow:**
- In the app, a customer can upgrade from Standard to Pro at any time
- Upgrade is instant — new features appear immediately after payment
- The price difference is prorated for the remaining months of the current billing period

**Downgrade flow:**
- Customers can downgrade from Pro to Standard
- Features are removed at the start of the next billing cycle
- A downgrade prompt asks why — the answer feeds into the product improvement roadmap

**Cancellation:**
- Customers can cancel any time from the app
- On cancellation, the bed reverts to Free tier features
- A win-back campaign (email + WhatsApp) is triggered at 7 days and 30 days after cancellation

## The Customer Journey — A Kuwait Family Example

Here is the complete journey of a real customer in Kuwait, from first discovery to year two of use:

---

**Month 0 — Discovery**
Khalid sees a TikTok video of the Smart Bed AI's LED sunrise with Arabic Fajr wake-up audio. He shares it in his family WhatsApp group. His wife Maryam is immediately interested — their current alarm wakes the baby every morning. He saves the link.

**Month 0 — Research**
Khalid visits the website. He watches the product video. He reads the Islamic features page carefully — Fajr, Ramadan mode, Tahajjud. He messages the WhatsApp sales agent to ask: *"Does it work if my wife and I have different Fajr times?"* The agent confirms partner mode handles this. He is convinced.

**Month 0 — Purchase**
Khalid orders the King-size Smart Bed AI with a 1-year Standard subscription as a bundle. He pays by credit card. He receives a confirmation message and an installation booking for 5 days later.

**Month 0 — Installation Day**
Two technicians arrive on time. The bed is installed in 70 minutes. Khalid sets up his profile, Maryam sets up hers. They do a voice test together. Danah greets them both by name and sets their Fajr times to their local mosque's schedule. The technician leaves a QR code for support.

**Week 1 — First Experience**
First night: the wind-down routine starts at 10 PM. The lights in the room slowly shift to warm amber. Danah says softly: *"Good evening, Khalid. Tonight, let's wind down together."* He falls asleep faster than usual.

4:45 AM: the lights on Khalid's side begin their sunrise. Maryam's side stays dark. He wakes for Fajr without an alarm going off. He feels calm. This is the first time in months he has woken for Fajr without his phone alarm waking Maryam too.

**Week 2 — Check-in**
A support agent messages on WhatsApp: *"Hi Khalid, how is the first week going? Any questions about setting up?"* Khalid mentions the Spotify integration doesn't work yet — the agent walks him through it in 5 minutes.

**Month 1 — Settled Routine**
Khalid and Maryam are now using the product every day. Their combined sleep score went from 59 to 71. Maryam upgraded to Pro because she wants the detailed weekly PDF reports to share with her doctor (she has mild insomnia).

**Month 3 — Ramadan**
Danah activates Ramadan mode automatically. She wakes them both for Suhoor at 3:30 AM with a different, gentler scene (soft blue lights, no music, just a quiet dua). Danah reduces her conversation frequency during the day out of respect for fasting. She sends them Quran verses each morning. Both say this is the best Ramadan they have had in years.

**Month 12 — Renewal**
Annual renewal reminder arrives. Maryam renews the Pro tier without hesitation. Khalid renews Standard. They tell three friends about the product. Two of them buy the bed.

## How the Operations Team Is Structured

For a startup phase (0–500 customers), the team structure is lean:

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Operations Manager** | Oversees installation, logistics, inventory |
| **Installation Technicians (2–3)** | In-home delivery and setup in Kuwait |
| **Customer Support Agents (2)** | WhatsApp and email support, onboarding calls |
| **Content Manager** | WhatsApp notifications, social media, Islamic content updates |
| **Sales Agent (1–2)** | WhatsApp sales conversations, B2B outreach |

As the customer base grows, support and installation teams scale accordingly. The AI-powered product is designed to reduce support load — most questions are answered by Danah or the in-app FAQ, not a human agent.
